// Core Concepts

The stuff that actually matters.

Three concepts are free. One is locked — spend 1 sat to unlock it. Consider it a proof-of-attention.

01 — FREE

The Blockchain

A public ledger of every transaction ever made, replicated across thousands of computers worldwide. Nobody owns it. Nobody can edit it without redoing years of computational work.

It's basically a shared Google Doc that 15,000 strangers are watching simultaneously and will riot if you change anything.

IMMUTABLE LEDGER

02 — FREE

Proof of Work

Miners compete to solve a mathematical puzzle. The winner adds the next block of transactions and earns the block reward. This costs real electricity — which is precisely the point. It makes cheating expensive.

To rewrite Bitcoin's history you'd need more computing power than every nation combined. Good luck.

SECURITY VIA ENERGY

03 — FREE

The Halving

Every 210,000 blocks (~4 years), the reward miners earn for adding a block is cut in half. The most recent halving was April 2024 — reward dropped from 6.25 to 3.125 BTC.

Next halving: ~April 2028. Reward drops to 1.5625 BTC. The last Bitcoin will be mined around 2140. Set a reminder.

NEXT: ~APR 2028

04 — ⚡ LOCKED

Self-Custody

This concept explains why "not your keys, not your coins" is the most important sentence in Bitcoin. Exchanges get hacked. Banks freeze accounts. Hardware wallets do neither.

When you hold your own private keys, no government, no company, and no one else can touch your sats.

YOUR KEYS YOUR COINS

// The Lightning Network

Bitcoin at the speed of light.

The base layer is intentionally slow — security first. Lightning is a second layer where payments settle in milliseconds for fractions of a cent. Unlimited transactions, one on-chain settlement.
